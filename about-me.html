Short Bio

I'm old enough that I'm starting to feel a little bit like Forrest Gump. I'm too young to have experienced Watergate and the moon landing, but I remember where I was when I found out the Challenger exploded. I worked on a Y2K project. I was living in NYC on 9/11.

I worked for The Associated Press for more than a decade as an election coordinator, compiling election data, populating state databases and building tabulation tables, and supervising state-level election night operations. The technology was really neat, and my peers were amazing... but it was extremely stressful.

Education

I graduated with an undergraduate degree in Classics from Reed College. Ten years after that, I left my full-time job to go back to school to study graphic design. Ten years after that, I spent a year at the University of Washington studying natural science illustration, and then a year at USC (online) getting a graduate certificate in GIS.

Now I'm working on an MLIS through San Jose State University (post-coordinate controlled vocabulary, anyone?), along with a certificate in front-end development (this program!).

Volunteering

I was freelancing as a graphic designer (and losing interest fast) when Covid shut everything down, including whatever work I had trickling in. (It was kind of a relief, actually.) Thankfully, I had been volunteering as a raptor docent at my local outdoor learning center for many years, and because that involves caring for live animals, I consistently had a place to go and something that needed to be done.

I love working with unreleasable raptors, and other volunteers, and working with the birds doing community outreach.

I became a Montana Master Naturalist last year (thanks to Zoom)!

Nature Journaling as a Master Naturalist

One focus of my master naturalist program was keeping a nature journal. I ended up hand-binding my journal and making a cover.

Winter 2022-2023 has been quite cold and snowy -- I did not see my first snowdrops until the end of March! I'm looking forward to warmer weather and spending time outside.
