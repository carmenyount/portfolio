Goals for this program and beyond:

When I was first exposed to HTML, it was in the days when we were still using QuarkXpress and appropriating tables to create page layouts for the web. Cascading Style Sheets were a revollutionary thing, but they weren't a widely used thing. (Those of us who were deep in Quark and transitioning to InDesign were psyched, because as it turns out, a style sheet is a style sheet is a style sheet. In other words, when people say "1996 called -- it wants its website back," I actually remember what that website looked like... and it was not pretty.

I went on to into print design and production and left the web behind (except as a user).

Fast forward to now... many years later... I'm an MLIS student at SJSUand a group I was working with had to use Caspio to build a small database for a class. As it turns out, interacting with Caspio works way better if you have a grasp of Javascript, which I don't. (No one in my group did, and it wasn't the point of the class, but it sure would have been helpful.)

I have a deep and abiding appreciation for information science. It's essential that people have access to information in ways that make sense for them.

And just about everything these days is web-based, so in order to be an effective information professional, I probably should be as well. Ultimately, I would like to be a front-end developer specializing in content management, but I have a whole bunch of other interests, including natural science illustration and nature journaling, so we'll see where all of this ends up...

(Also, apparently this skill set is somewhat in demand out in the "real world.")
